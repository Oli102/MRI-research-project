Demonstration

The Experiment: Sweeping a strong neodymium magnet over a grid of mini-compasses.

  • Alignment: The compasses represent hydrogen protons in the body. When the strong magnet comes close, they all snap into alignment, just like the MRI's main electromagnet.
  • Resonance: Tapping the compasses or sweeping a smaller magnet past them demonstrates the Radio Frequency (RF) pulse temporarily knocking the protons out of alignment.
